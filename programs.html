PROGRAMS

CHUI

PLAY GROUP

2½ - 3 Years

Watch Out!

I copy a lot, especially from adults and older children. I am excited when with other children, sharing might be a problem with me sometimes mine is mine. I am defiant sometimes and will do what I have been told not to do. I enjoy chase games with other children.

What I need

I need space to explore. I need other children to play with.

An opportunity to run and chase.

I just need you to be patient with me.

Twiga

Baby Class

3 - 4 Years

Watch Out!

I am charged with physical energy. I do things on my own terms. My mind is a sponge. Reading and socializing are essential in getting me ready for school. I like to pretend a lot and enjoy scribbling on everything. I am full of questions, many of which are "Why?"

I become fairly reliable about using the potty. I may stay dry at night and may not. Playing and trying new things out are how I learn. Sometimes I like to share. I begin to listen more and begin to understand how to solve problems for myself.

What I need

I want to know about everything and understand words and when encouraged, I will use words instead of grabbing, crying or pushing. Play with me, sing to me and let's pretend.

Ndovu

Pre-Primary 1

4 - 5 Years

Watch Out!

I'm in an active stage,running,hopping, jumping and climbing. I love to ask "Why?" and "How?" I'm interested in numbers and the world around me. I enjoy playing with my friends. I like to be creative with my drawings, and I may like my pictures to be different from everyone else's. I'm curious. I may want to be just like my older sister or brother. I am proud that I am so BIG now!

What I need

I need to explore, to try out, and to test limits. Giving me room to grow doesn't mean letting me do everything. I need reasonable limits set for my own protection and for others. Let me know clearly what is or isn't to be expected. I need to learn to give and take and play well with others. I need to be read to, talked to and listened to. I need to be given choices and to learn things in my own way. Label objects and describe what's happening to me so I can learn new words and things

Simba

Pre-Primary 2

5 - 6 Years

Watch Out!

I'm slowing a little in growth. I have good motor control, but my small muscles aren't as developed as my large muscles for jumping. My activity level is high and my play has direction. I like writing my name, drawing pictures, making projects and going to the library. I'm more interested now in doing group activities, sharing things and my feelings. I like quiet time away from the other kids from time to time. I may be anxious to begin kindergarten.

What I need

I need opportunities for plenty of active play. I need to do things for myself. I like to have choices in how I learn new things. But most of all, I need your love and assurance that I'm important. I need time, patience, understanding and genuine attention. I am learning about who I am and how I fit in with others. I need to know how I am doing in a positive way. I understand more about things and how they work, so you can give me a more detailed answer. I have a big imagination and pretend a lot. Although I'm becoming taller, your lap is still one of my favourite places.

Swara

Grade 1

6 - 7 Years

Watch Out!

I have improved hand-eye coordination for things like tying shoelaces. I gain fine motor control and can perform movements designed to develop strength and endurance. I can also perform activities that require less intense but continuous movements. My reasoning and logic skills are developing and I like to think before making decisions. I begin to have a better sense of time like days, months, years and seasons.

What I need

I may be more independent but less secure and may want a lot of attention from adults. Recognition of my accomplishments and my strengths builds my self-esteem and self-confidence. I need opportunities to explore and learn, inside and outside. I need patience from my care-givers as I tend to ask a lot of questions trying to understand the world around me.

Kifaru

Grade 2

7 - 8 Years

Watch Out!

My gross motor skills have really developed and I can play and be active for longer periods without getting tired. My strength in both big and small muscles have also improved and can run farther distances for longer durations. My understanding of cause and effect has improved and I clearly understand that there are consequences for my actions. I have a longer attention span and can do complex math like division and multiplication. I am also developing a sense of body image.

Nyati

Grade 3

8 - 9 Years

Watch Out!

My fine motor skills are getting better as I can write faster, smaller and more fluid. Enhanced fine motor skills and coordination improve my gross motor skills and activities like swimming and playing become more enjoyable. I am capable of adult-like speech and baby-talk of earlier years is mostly gone. I can also effectively express myself in writing. My ability to understand the concept of space in a more abstract way by visualizing objects in space and can draw conclusions about where an object will be or what it will look like from a different perspective.

What I need

Developmentally appropriate routines and activities by engaging in physical activities. I still need to be read aloud to as this promotes my literacy and language skills. However, I need to read independently each day to foster my love for books.

Mbuni

Grade 4

9 - 10 Years

Watch Out!

I am at an age where my development is packed with learning and rapid-paced cognitive growth. I start to sound more grown up as my ability to gather information and formulate well-organized opinions and thoughts has gone up by a level. Academically, I am expected to work with fractions, multiplication and division skills and can learn complex geometry concepts. Physically, my body is beginning to grow and change rapidly and might even start going through adolescence.

What I need

I need to spend quality time with my caregivers who are to listen to me more than they talk. I also need my privacy with friends as holding private conversations and sharing secrets is important for my early development.
